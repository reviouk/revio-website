Web Directory Submissions – Are They Still Effective?

29 July 2010

Brief Insight into Link Building from Directories

Many SEO companies submit client sites to web directories, though restraint is crucial. Indiscriminate submissions yield poor results.

The effective approach is selectivity. Submit to appropriate categories with keyword-rich title tags within your niche. Quality human-edited directories perform better than automated ones.

Example

For a used car sales site, target a directory category labeled "cars" or "used cars"—not "automobiles." Directory administrators should optimize category names for search relevance.

Are Web Directories Viable for Quality Links?

Both yes and no. They work when used judiciously as part of a diversified link profile including social media and niche blogs. Overreliance on directories undermines SEO performance.

Key considerations:

  • Assess outbound link volume on category pages
  • Check PageRank of linking pages
  • Excessive outbound links dilute link value

Maintain directory backlinks below 20% of total link volume for optimal results. Top Google rankings require superior strategies compared to competitors. If leading competitors avoid directory submissions in favor of niche-relevant links, directory-only strategies fall short.
