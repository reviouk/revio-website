How to Track Sales Conversions From Facebook

12 February 2015

Many advertisers run Facebook ads without understanding their effectiveness or sales impact. This guide covers Facebook conversion tracking and offsite pixel configuration to measure your Return On Investment (ROI).

While Google Analytics can track eCommerce and referral sources, it cannot identify which specific Facebook advertisement drove a particular conversion. The Facebook conversion code and offsite pixel solve this problem.

How Facebook Conversion Tracking Works and the Different Conversion Types

Facebook employs Optimised CPM (oCPM) technology to determine ad delivery. This system lets advertisers prioritise marketing objectives while automatically distributing ads for maximum effectiveness, helping optimise budget value.

Your advertisement targets people most likely to perform your desired action.

When configuring your offsite pixel, you can optimise for these conversion types:

  • Checkouts
  • Registrations
  • Leads
  • Key page views
  • Adds to cart
  • Other website conversions

Facebook learns conversion patterns from advertisers using tracking tools, using this intelligence to target ads toward high-probability converters.

Setting Up Facebook Ads and Conversion Tracking Step by Step

Power Editor offers superior Facebook ad management compared to Ads Manager. Ads Manager's requirement to create unique pixels per ad can prove confusing.

1. Start Facebook Power Editor

Access Power Editor from Ads Manager or visit: https://www.facebook.com/ads/manage/powereditor/

From there, create your offsite pixel under the conversion tracking settings, place the pixel code on your relevant confirmation or thank-you pages, and assign it to the conversion type that matches your campaign goal. Once live, Facebook's reporting will attribute conversions back to the specific ad and campaign responsible, giving you a true picture of ROI rather than a guess.
