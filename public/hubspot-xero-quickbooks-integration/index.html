How to Connect HubSpot to Xero or QuickBooks (and Why Your Sales Team Will Thank You)

4 April 2026

The Hidden Cost of Disconnected CRM and Accounting

Every business using both a CRM and accounting platform experiences friction. Your sales team closes a deal in HubSpot, then manual work begins — someone must transfer that information to Xero or QuickBooks so an invoice can be generated. This handoff is one of the most common sources of inefficiency, errors and frustration in growing businesses.

This guide explains how to connect HubSpot to Xero or QuickBooks, details the available options, and demonstrates why proper integration transforms revenue operations. Whether you use HubSpot Starter or a higher tier, you'll find immediately actionable advice.

The Specific Pain of Disconnected Systems

Delayed Invoicing

Manual handoff delays are inevitable. A Friday afternoon deal closure may go unnoticed until Monday, or longer if finance staff are unavailable. Every invoicing delay extends cash flow impact. Research shows faster post-deal invoice delivery accelerates payment receipt — a one-week delay can add two to three weeks to average payment cycles.

Data Entry Errors

Manual transfers between systems create inherent risks. Mistyped amounts, incorrect VAT rates, outdated addresses or missing purchase order numbers require correction and resending. In a survey of UK small businesses, 61 per cent reported that invoice errors had caused payment delays of two weeks or more.

No Visibility Across Teams

Separated CRM and accounting systems create silos. Sales teams cannot confirm invoicing status for closed deals. Finance teams lack deal context — promised deliverables, payment terms, billing contacts. Leadership cannot easily track the pipeline-to-revenue journey without manual, multi-system compilation.

Time Wasted Chasing Information

Without integration, teams spend substantial time gathering information. Finance managers request billing addresses from sales reps; reps contact clients; responses arrive days later. One REVIO client calculated that their team spent an average of 45 minutes per deal on this back-and-forth — time that was completely eliminated after integration.

What Does the Integration Actually Look Like?

A well-implemented HubSpot-to-Xero or HubSpot-to-QuickBooks integration follows this typical flow:

  • Step 1: A sales rep moves a deal to "Closed Won" in HubSpot, containing client name, deal value, line items, payment terms, billing contact and custom properties.
  • Step 2: The integration detects this stage change and triggers automatically — no manual action required.
  • Step 3: The integration checks whether the client already exists in Xero or QuickBooks. Existing records are matched; new clients create fresh contacts.
  • Step 4: A draft or sent invoice is created in the accounting platform, populated with deal value, line items, VAT rate, payment terms, due date and reference numbers from HubSpot.
  • Step 5: Optionally, the HubSpot deal record updates with the invoice number and accounting system link, giving instant invoicing status visibility.
  • Step 6: Notifications trigger — Slack messages to finance channels, or emails to billing contacts confirming invoice creation.

Three Setup Options: Native, Middleware and Custom

Option 1: Native Marketplace App

Both Xero and QuickBooks offer native HubSpot App Marketplace integrations for basic syncing.

Pros: quick setup (typically under 30 minutes), no additional subscription costs beyond existing tools, regular maintenance by developers, and reliability for standard scenarios.

Cons: limited customisation of field mapping, sync triggers or data transformation. Some require higher HubSpot tiers. Custom properties and complex rules may be unsupported.

Best for: businesses with straightforward invoicing processes and no unusual data mapping requirements.

Option 2: Zapier or Make (Recommended for HubSpot Starter)

Middleware platforms enable custom integration logic without coding, offering flexibility that's especially useful for HubSpot Starter users.

Pros: highly customisable field mapping, trigger timing and conditions. Works with HubSpot Starter without upgrades. Supports multi-step automations including notifications and spreadsheet updates. Thousands of additional app connections, with visual no-code builders non-technical staff can understand.

Cons: requires a Zapier or Make subscription (typically £20–£100 monthly depending on volume), demands initial configuration time, and lower-tier plans impose task limits that can constrain high-volume businesses.

Best for: HubSpot Starter users needing flexible, customised automation without a plan upgrade.

Option 3: Custom API Integration

Complex requirements — high transaction volumes, custom ERP systems, multi-entity accounting structures or regulatory compliance needs — may necessitate a custom API integration.

Pros: complete control over every aspect of the integration, handling any complexity level, performing reliably at scale, and connecting to proprietary or legacy systems.

Cons: requires developer expertise (typical costs £1,500–£5,000+), ongoing maintenance as APIs evolve, and longer implementation timelines (weeks rather than hours).

Best for: businesses with complex, high-volume or highly regulated invoicing requirements.

Step-by-Step: Connecting HubSpot Deals to Xero Invoices via Zapier

This detailed walkthrough covers the most common Zapier implementation; Make follows similar concepts with a different interface.

  • Step 1 — Create a Zapier account and connect your apps: sign up for Zapier if needed, create a new Zap, and connect HubSpot (OAuth login) and Xero (OAuth-based connection).
  • Step 2 — Set the trigger: choose HubSpot as the trigger app and select "Deal Stage Changed", configured for "Closed Won" (or your equivalent). Test the trigger to verify Zapier retrieves sample deal records.
  • Step 3 — Add a filter (optional but recommended): ensure the Zap runs only for qualifying deals — those with values above £0, specific pipelines, or an "Invoice Required" property marked "Yes".
  • Step 4 — Search for the contact in Xero: add a "Find Contact" action, mapping client name or email from HubSpot to search existing Xero contacts and prevent duplicates.
  • Step 5 — Create the contact if not found: add conditional logic to create a new Xero contact from HubSpot client details (name, email, address, phone) if none exists.
  • Step 6 — Create the invoice: add a "Create Invoice" action in Xero, mapping contact, invoice date, due date, line item description, amount, tax rate and reference number from the HubSpot deal.
  • Step 7 — Update the HubSpot deal: write the Xero invoice number back to a custom deal property so sales teams see invoicing status without leaving the CRM.
  • Step 8 — Test and activate: run the complete Zap with test data, verify correct invoice creation and deal updates, then activate.

Data Mapping: HubSpot Fields to Xero and QuickBooks Fields

  • Associated Company Name → Xero: Contact Name / QuickBooks: Customer Name
  • Deal Amount → Xero: Invoice Line Amount / QuickBooks: Invoice Line Amount
  • Close Date → Xero: Invoice Date / QuickBooks: Invoice Date
  • Payment Terms (custom) → Xero: Due Date / Terms / QuickBooks: Terms
  • Line Items → Xero: Invoice Line Items / QuickBooks: Invoice Line Items
  • VAT Rate (custom) → Xero: Tax Rate / QuickBooks: Tax Code
  • PO Number (custom) → Xero: Reference / QuickBooks: PO Number
  • Associated Contact Email → Xero: Contact Email / QuickBooks: Customer Email
  • Billing Address (custom) → Xero: Contact Address / QuickBooks: Billing Address

Some fields — particularly payment terms, VAT rate, PO number and billing address — may need creating as custom HubSpot properties if they don't already exist. REVIO assists with designing proper property structures.

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

  • Duplicate contacts: always include a "search before create" step. Without it, every invoice creates a new accounting contact, resulting in painful duplicate accumulation.
  • Missing or incorrect VAT rates: ensure reliable VAT rate properties on HubSpot deals, and set default values in Zapier or Make to prevent zero-VAT invoices.
  • Currency mismatches: verify HubSpot deal currency aligns with Xero or QuickBooks expectations, as mismatches cause invoice creation failures.
  • Line item complexity: single-line deals are straightforward, but multiple line items with varying quantities and tax rates require automation that handles arrays — Make typically handles this more elegantly than Zapier.
  • Test with real data: before activation, run the automation with several real or realistic deals to verify complete field mapping and edge cases.
  • Error notifications: configure alerts in Zapier or Make so failures are noticed immediately — silent failures are worse than manual processes.

When to Choose Native vs Middleware vs Custom

Use native integration for simple invoicing processes without custom field mapping needs, where pre-built connectors provide complete functionality.

Use Zapier or Make on HubSpot Starter when you need custom field mapping, multi-step automation, or connections to additional tools within a single process. This suits most growing businesses.

Use a custom API integration for complex multi-entity accounting structures, extremely high transaction volumes, regulatory or compliance requirements, or proprietary system connections lacking existing connectors.
