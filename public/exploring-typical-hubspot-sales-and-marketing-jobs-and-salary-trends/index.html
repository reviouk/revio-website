Exploring Typical HubSpot Consultant Sales and Marketing Jobs and Salary Trends

3 July 2023

The world of sales and marketing has witnessed a significant transformation with the advent of technology and automation. HubSpot, a renowned inbound marketing and sales platform, offers a range of job opportunities across various domains. In this article, we will delve into the typical roles of HubSpot consultant sales and marketing professionals, with a particular focus on Channel Manager and CRM Manager positions. Additionally, we will explore the salary trends associated with these roles in the United Kingdom (UK).

1. HubSpot Sales Jobs

1.1 Channel Manager

Channel Managers play a crucial role in managing relationships with partner channels, such as resellers and distributors, to drive revenue growth. They collaborate with channel partners to develop joint marketing strategies, provide training and support, and ensure the effective utilisation of HubSpot's sales tools. Channel Managers also analyse sales performance, identify new business opportunities, and build strong relationships with partners.

Salary Range (UK): The salary for Channel Managers in the UK can vary based on factors such as experience, industry, and the size of the partner network. On average, a HubSpot Channel Manager in the UK can expect a salary range of £40,000 to £60,000 per year. However, this can go higher for experienced professionals or those managing larger partner networks.

1.2 HubSpot CRM Manager

Customer Relationship Management (CRM) Managers are responsible for implementing and managing CRM systems, including HubSpot's CRM platform. They collaborate with sales and marketing teams to ensure seamless integration, data accuracy, and efficient use of CRM tools. CRM Managers also provide training and support to users, analyse data and metrics to optimise processes, and drive user adoption within the organisation.

Salary Range (UK): The salary for CRM Managers in the UK can vary based on factors such as experience, industry, and the complexity of the CRM implementation. On average, a CRM Manager specialising in HubSpot in the UK can earn between £35,000 and £55,000 per year.

2. HubSpot Marketing Jobs

2.1 Marketing Manager

Marketing Managers play a pivotal role in developing and executing marketing strategies to drive brand awareness, lead generation, and customer engagement. They analyse market trends, identify target audiences, create marketing plans, manage budgets, and oversee campaign performance. Marketing Managers leverage HubSpot's marketing tools to automate workflows, track campaign metrics, and nurture leads.

Salary Range (UK): The salary for Marketing Managers in the UK varies based on factors such as experience, industry, and company size. On average, a Marketing Manager specialising in HubSpot in the UK can earn between £40,000 and £60,000 per year.

2.2 HubSpot Marketing Specialist

Marketing Specialists focus on implementing and optimising marketing campaigns, leveraging HubSpot's marketing automation capabilities. They create and manage content, execute email marketing campaigns, analyse campaign performance, and drive lead generation efforts. Marketing Specialists collaborate with cross-functional teams to align messaging and deliver impactful marketing initiatives.

Salary Range (UK): The salary for Marketing Specialists in the UK can vary based on factors such as experience, industry, and the scope of responsibilities. On average, a Marketing Specialist specialising in HubSpot in the UK can expect a salary range of £30,000 to £45,000 per year.

Conclusion

HubSpot offers a diverse range of sales and marketing roles that are instrumental in driving business growth and success. Channel Managers play a critical role in managing partner relationships, while CRM Managers ensure efficient utilisation of the CRM platform. Marketing Managers and Marketing Specialists focus on developing and executing effective marketing strategies. In the UK, salaries for these positions vary based on factors such as experience, industry, and company size. It is important for employers and employees to consider these factors when determining appropriate compensation.
