The Complete Guide to HubSpot Custom Integrations for Growing Businesses

5 April 2026

Introduction: Why HubSpot Integrations Matter More Than Ever

Most growing businesses rely on a diverse ecosystem of tools — accounting software, project management platforms, communication apps, and e-commerce systems. The challenge is ensuring these tools communicate seamlessly, allowing teams to focus on revenue-generating activities rather than manual data entry.

HubSpot integrations connect your CRM with other software, enabling automatic data flow. The right integrations transform disconnected tools into a unified operating system for your business.

What Are HubSpot Integrations?

A HubSpot integration is any connection between HubSpot and another application that allows data to flow automatically, reliably, and in real time or near-real time. Three broad categories exist:

1. Native Marketplace Integrations

HubSpot's App Marketplace contains over 1,500 pre-built integrations developed by HubSpot or third-party vendors. These require minimal setup — typically just authorisation and basic configuration. Developers maintain them with regular updates and API compliance testing.

Examples include:

  • Native Slack integration (pushes CRM notifications to Slack channels)
  • Google Workspace integration (logs emails and calendar events)
  • Xero integration (syncs invoice data)

These integrations work well for standard use cases but offer limited customisation options regarding field selection, sync timing, and data transformation.

2. Middleware Integrations (Zapier and Make)

Middleware platforms like Zapier and Make bridge HubSpot and virtually any application. You define the logic: when a specific event occurs in HubSpot (deal creation, property changes, form submissions), the middleware triggers actions in another tool.

This approach particularly benefits HubSpot Starter users, who lack built-in automation features like workflows or sequences. Zapier and Make provide powerful automation capabilities without requiring tier upgrades. You can build multi-step automations, add conditional logic, format data, and connect to thousands of applications — all without coding.

3. Custom API Integrations

For highly specific or complex requirements, custom API integrations offer maximum flexibility. These bespoke connections, built by developers using HubSpot's REST API and other APIs, handle advanced data transformations, large record volumes, complex business rules, and connections to proprietary or legacy systems.

The trade-off involves cost and maintenance. Custom integrations require development expertise to build and ongoing support as APIs evolve. They suit businesses with unique processes that native or middleware options cannot replicate.

The Most Common HubSpot Integrations

Xero and QuickBooks (Accounting)

Connecting your CRM to accounting platforms creates high-impact integration. When deals close in HubSpot, invoices automatically generate in Xero or QuickBooks with correct client details, line items, and payment terms. This eliminates manual handoffs between sales and finance, reduces errors, and accelerates cash flow.

For HubSpot Starter users, Zapier or Make is recommended, allowing direct mapping of deal properties to invoice fields.

Slack (Team Communication)

Slack integrations keep teams informed in real time. Push deal stage changes, form submissions, support ticket updates, and task reminders directly to dedicated channels. This ensures sales teams see high-value deal movements, support teams receive VIP customer alerts, and marketing teams get instant campaign submission notifications.

The native HubSpot-Slack integration handles basic notifications, while Zapier or Make enables more customised notification logic.

Google Workspace (Email and Calendar)

The Google Workspace integration logs emails sent and received through Gmail against relevant HubSpot contact records, providing full communication history visibility. Calendar events sync between HubSpot's meeting tool and Google Calendar. This native, free integration should be among the first connections every HubSpot user enables.

Zapier and Make (Automation Layer)

These middleware platforms serve as universal connectors. Beyond simple point-to-point connections, they enable multi-step automations spanning several tools. Example: a new HubSpot form submission triggers automation that creates a contact in HubSpot, adds a Google Sheet row for reporting, sends personalised Slack notification to the sales rep, and creates a follow-up task in Asana — all from a single trigger.

Asana, Monday.com, and Trello (Project Management)

When deals close, closed deals automatically generate projects, tasks, or boards in delivery platforms. This eliminates the gap between sales and operations, reduces dropped handoff risks, and gives delivery teams immediate access to client context captured during sales.

Shopify and WooCommerce (E-Commerce)

For product-selling businesses, connecting e-commerce platforms to HubSpot unlocks customer insights. Purchase history, order values, abandoned carts, and product preferences flow into HubSpot, enabling customer segmentation, personalised communications, and upsell opportunity identification.

The native Shopify integration is robust, while WooCommerce connections typically require Zapier, Make, or custom solutions.

When Do You Need Integrations? Detailed Scenarios

Scenario 1: The Manual Invoice Bottleneck

Sales teams close deals in HubSpot, then email finance teams with details. Finance manually creates invoices in Xero — sometimes days later. Errors emerge: wrong amounts, missing purchase orders, incorrect VAT rates. Cash flow suffers from delayed invoicing. Integration eliminates this by automatically generating invoices upon deal closure.

Scenario 2: The Invisible Sales Pipeline

Leadership wants real-time pipeline visibility, but sales data lives in HubSpot, financial data in Xero, and project data in Asana. Nobody sees the complete picture. Integrations unify these sources, enabling dashboards and reports showing the journey from lead to revenue to delivery.

Scenario 3: The Onboarding Gap

New client sign-ups create chaotic handoffs from sales to delivery. Delivery teams lack clarity on promises, timelines, or key contacts. Integrating HubSpot with project management tools automatically creates projects pre-populated with relevant details and assigned to correct team members.

Scenario 4: The Data Silo Problem

Marketing uses HubSpot, sales uses HubSpot, support uses separate tools, and finance uses another. Each team holds partial customer views. Integrations break down silos, ensuring every team accesses complete customer pictures.
