Facebook Ads Revamped for Small Businesses: we all want to be liked

19 December 2012

Facebook has introduced a simplified advertising strategy with three primary options for small business promotion. The platform now enables companies to choose between growing their audience through page likes, promoting individual page posts for increased engagement, or utilising advanced bidding options.

Get More Page Likes

The "Get More Page Likes" feature includes a landing view option, allowing advertisers to direct customers to specific destinations – timelines, events, or custom apps – facilitating A/B testing across different Facebook properties.

Sponsored stories appear within news feeds marked as "Sponsored" in subtle grey text. These posts receive higher placement in feeds compared to standard organic posts. The new "promote Page Posts" capability enables businesses to boost visibility for underperforming or strategic content like competitions or industry updates.

Targeting Capabilities

Advanced targeting options include custom interests and sub-categories. Advertisers can exclude audiences already familiar with their brand and target individuals connected through apps, pages, or events. The platform offers granular control through "Target people who are connected to" or "Target people who are not connected to" parameters.

Strategic Considerations

Facebook remains fundamentally a social engagement platform rather than a search-driven marketplace for products or services. While unsuitable for aggressive sales approaches or high-volume lead generation, it effectively builds local business connections and strengthens brand presence in communities.
