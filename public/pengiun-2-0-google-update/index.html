Pengiun 2.0 Google Update: how to approach SEO now?

26 May 2013

Webmasters and online business owners have been holding their breath in anticipation of the latest Google Penguin 2.0 update, which is the next generation algorithm change designed to combat web spam. Not to be confused with the SEO industry's own naming convention and the 4th Penguin roll out, Penguin 2.0 is Google's internal name for it – regardless of the tag, it has the same weighted message to those who are still in the high risk game of buying links.

According to Matt Cutts, head of web spam at Google: "It's going to have a pretty big impact on web spam... It's a brand new generation of algorithms. The previous iteration of Penguin would essentially only look at the home page of a website. The newer generation of Penguin goes much deeper and has a really big impact in specific areas."

Like martial arts philosophy that flows naturally without rigid styles, link building should follow organic principles. Google invests heavily in detecting black hat and grey hat SEO tactics. This reinforces that SEO depends fundamentally on content marketing and social media.

So what types of links are acceptable now?

Google has substantially increased its capacity to detect and penalise unnatural link-building practices. Quality links should be:

  • Citations that are natural and relevant to source material
  • External references that serve genuine purposes
  • From reputable sites (edu sites historically valued, but only if relevant)

If continuing manual link building, strategy must involve:

  • Reduced reliance on keyword-rich anchor text
  • Elimination of site-wide links
  • Wide variety of relevant citations
  • Interesting, engaging content

So what does this mean for guest posting and guest posting platforms?

Guest posting works as a supplemental strategy when executed properly. However, considerations include:

For your own site: Accept guest posts responsibly by clearly marking content/links as advertising or sponsorship with no-follow attributes. Non-related outbound do-follow blogroll links appear suspicious to Google, which penalises both source and destination sites.

For guest posting platforms: Be cautious with free or paid platforms where you have limited editorial control. Forced bio profiles with anchor text at article bottoms – often justified by Google Author Rank models – have become prevalent among SEO practitioners and warrant careful evaluation. Future updates may scrutinise these methods more closely.

Key insight: If guest posting serves as your primary strategy purely for convenience, shortcuts eventually backfire. Prioritise sites with highly respected editorial policies and strong search engine trust. Historical guest posting pursued solely for link acquisition with rich anchor text likely involved low-trust sites.

The Only Way to SEO Is Content Marketing

Respected digital marketers have evolved link building into genuine SEO and search marketing tactics over recent years. Content marketing is not new but has traditionally been overlooked due to implementation complexity.

Creating exceptional content requires more than quality media (images, videos, editorial-standard articles). It demands:

  • Genuine audience connection and engagement
  • Active social media participation
  • Brand management and authority building
  • Strategic content planning and creation

This approach generates the type of links Google values – organic references from audiences wanting to cite your work. Business owners lacking provisions for media content creation and integrated social media strategy should reconsider their SEO approach before it's too late.
