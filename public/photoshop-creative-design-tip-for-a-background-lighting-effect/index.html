Photoshop Creative Design Tip For a Background Lighting Effect

9 December 2012

Sometimes the best visual Photoshop effects are discovered through hours of experimentation and creative ad-libing.

The Technique Overview

This straightforward method involves layering a coloured background image beneath your primary image to create atmospheric lighting effects.

Step 1: Find a Background Image

Select an image featuring compelling lighting qualities – for instance, a nighttime photograph with warm colour tones that could simulate sunset ambience. The example uses a long-exposure shot of London with car headlights, enhanced with a warm magenta filter.

Step 2: Add Your Image

Place your primary photograph as the top layer in Photoshop. The guide demonstrates using an arctic reindeer photo positioned above the lighting-effect background layer.

Step 3: The Magic Effect

Adjust the opacity of your top layer to allow the textured background to blend through. The tutorial applies 70% opacity to create a merging effect between layers.

Step 4: The Final Result

The blended composition combines the reindeer photograph with the warm lighting undertones, producing an atmospheric sunset effect over the arctic scene.
