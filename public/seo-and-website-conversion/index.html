SEO and Website Conversion – the Big Brands

21 December 2009

More and more website owners are achieving strong SEO rankings but failing to capitalise on their traffic potential and generate meaningful revenue.

While ranking number one on Google and other search engines is valuable, it doesn't automatically guarantee business success.

Search engine optimisation firms excel at securing rankings, but business owners must also understand their industry, competition, and market position. Success requires differentiation – whether through free shipping, competitive pricing, or products solving real problems. Simply achieving top positions doesn't guarantee sales conversions.

Many entrepreneurs launch similar businesses in saturated markets, assuming premium rankings will drive substantial revenue. Building a recognised, trustworthy brand has become essential for competing online.

This challenge intensifies when competing against established retailers with in-house marketing teams and substantial budgets. Newer online businesses face additional difficulty, especially with Google's personalised search favouring established brands, disadvantaging smaller and mid-sized companies. Website design significantly impacts success – clean layouts, intuitive navigation, and trust-building elements matter throughout the customer journey.

The example of dreams.co.uk demonstrates effective online retail strategy: their site features simple navigation and accessible customer service links. Their high street presence builds familiarity. However, smaller competitors must excel in all these areas to survive in today's challenging economy.

Success extends beyond SEO alone. Comprehensive search marketing encompasses multiple interconnected elements: SEO, conversion optimisation, content and behavioural analysis, analytics, PPC advertising, direct marketing channels, affiliate marketing, social media optimisation, and attribution management. These channels require coordinated examination to identify which strategies drive conversions.
