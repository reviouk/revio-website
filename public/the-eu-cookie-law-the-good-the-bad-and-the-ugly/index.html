The EU Cookie Law Compliance: The Good, The Bad and The Ugly!

24 July 2012

What is a Cookie?

A cookie is a common web technology for remembering who you are between web pages. These enable login functionality, user preferences, visitor tracking, and targeted advertising. Approximately 92% of websites utilise cookies.

The Law's Requirements

The EU cookie regulation mandates that websites obtain visitor consent before deploying cookies through dropdown or accordion-style notification messages with acceptance/rejection options. Exceptions exist for certain cookie types, though analytics and advertising cookies face restrictions. This privacy protection framework allows users to control personal data usage.

Legal Background

A 2009 EU directive established the cookie consent requirement, compelling member states to implement national legislation by May 2011. Only three countries met this deadline; the UK delayed enforcement by one year following partial initial compliance. The deadline for full implementation was May 26, 2012.

Implementation Options

Three approaches exist:

  1. Ignore the requirement – risking future penalties
  2. Eliminate cookies entirely – impractical given technology dependencies
  3. Deploy a permission solution – the recommended path

Technical Solutions

Two main implementation approaches include popup notifications (often blocked by browsers) or accordion panels sliding from the page top. Solutions require developer implementation or self-installation using available free scripts.
