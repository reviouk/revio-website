Have You Ever Outsourced to an Offshore or Indian SEO Company?

18 January 2012

Introduction

If you've hired offshore SEO services, you're likely either wallowing around in a pitiful mess of failed campaign promises or haven't yet realised the consequences of your choice.

Conduct thorough research before dismissing this perspective – while some legitimate offshore providers exist, finding quality ones resembles locating a needle in a haystack. The fundamental principle applies: you generally receive what you pay for.

Outsourcing your link building to a far-away, hourly SEO contractor with no knowledge of your company is a good decision and A1 strategy for your business? … No IT's…REALLY NOT!

Common Warning Signs

Do any of these situations describe your experience?

  • Keyword rankings underperform expectations
  • Uncertainty about backlink quality or quantity received monthly
  • Generic link-building packages (e.g. 50 blog links, 100 article directory submissions, 500 directory submissions, 300 social bookmarks, 50 forum links)
  • SEO providers utilising spammy tactics (free article submissions, automated methods)
  • Vague monthly reports lacking transparency
  • Poor return on investment
  • Monthly payments with minimal results
  • Consideration of switching providers

10 Facts About Offshore Link Building Companies

  1. Bulk Automation: High-volume directory submissions don't constitute a viable strategy despite promises of thousands of monthly links.
  2. Low-Quality Networks: Offshore providers maintain self-built site portfolios featuring poor content, minimal authority, low Google PageRank, identical WordPress templates, spelling errors, and cross-linking patterns.
  3. Inflated Metrics: Social bookmarking claims lack credibility – few quality sites remain. Bulk submission tools targeting Pligg sites cause damage rather than benefit.
  4. Contractor Concerns: Platforms like oDesk offer disappointing results, with providers spending time spinning content for worthless article directories. Exercise caution regarding fake testimonials.
  5. Tool Limitations: Most cannot afford professional SEO software or identify high-value advertising placements.
  6. Link Wheel Strategies: While potentially useful when combined with quality backlinks, these methods alone waste resources without competitive results.
  7. Profile Damage: Offshore providers risk damaging backlink profiles, potentially demoting websites from Google visibility permanently.
  8. Ineffective Keywords: First-page rankings for low-competition long-tail keywords generate negligible traffic compared to competitive keyword targeting.
  9. Outdated Methods: Most offshore personnel operate solo with minimal SEO training, employing yesterday's tactics.
  10. Free Method Reliance: Rock-bottom pricing reflects deployment of completely free SEO techniques, with providers feeling minimal guilt when damaging rankings.
