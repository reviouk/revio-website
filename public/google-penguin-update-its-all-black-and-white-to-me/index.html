Google Penguin Update: it's all Black and White to Me..?

5 May 2012

If you're one of the many websites affected by Google's algorithm change on Tuesday, April 24th, 2012, you've likely been hit by the "Penguin update." The name references black and white imagery, symbolizing black hat and white hat SEO strategies—bad versus good practices respectively. While some consider this a minor change compared to the Panda release, business owners experiencing ranking drops and revenue loss would argue otherwise.

Why Has Google Implemented the Penguin Update?

Google deployed this update to detect and combat web spam and poor-quality websites. The change pushes low-quality sites into obscurity off search engine results. Only 3% of search queries were reportedly affected, though debate continues about whether quality sites were inadvertently filtered. The fundamental message: stop competing against Google's algorithms unless you possess exceptional expertise.

Panda or Penguin – Which Has My Website Been Hit By?

Examine your keyword ranking timeline alongside analytics data to determine impact timing. Rankings dropping before April 24th suggest Panda influence, while drops after indicate Penguin impact.

Panda 3.5 targeted poor-quality pages with substandard content—poorly written keyword-rich material, duplicated text, spammy advertisements. Penguin focused on web spam: over-optimization through backlinking and excessive anchor text usage, including blog networks and links from spammy sources.

There's Been Plenty of Warning and Information: Is It Game Over?

Google pursued search experience perfection for years, providing numerous signals. Webmasters using pure white hat techniques achieved results, while some employing marginal black and grey hat methods succeeded equally. The update represents "check mate" for many link builders and spam merchants.

Have You Been Participating in Any of These Link Building Methods?

1. Lazy Link Building Techniques: Link Buying or Brokering

Site-wide links now carry significantly less weight.

  • Non-related blogroll links using site-wide links with or without anchor text receive harsher treatment. Natural linking between unrelated industries (fashion to automotive) doesn't occur organically.
  • Related site blogroll links using keyword-rich anchor text warrant scrutiny. Green energy blog owners might reference related sites but wouldn't use keyword-dense anchor text, signaling Google's sophisticated analysis.

2. Poor Quality Articles Freighted with Anchor Text

Submitting articles with 2-3 keyword-rich links to your site appears unacceptable. Editorial credits should feel natural, with publishers choosing your company name rather than keyword phrases.

3. Purpose-Built SEO Blogs

Many high-ranked sites link from PR Zero pages built superficially, a major Penguin target.

4. Automated Spinning Article Software and Submission Networks

Automated approaches receive penalization.

5. Non-Related Reciprocal Linking from Spammy Resources

6. Related Site Reciprocal Linking from Spammy Resource Pages

This practice commonly appears in competitor backlink profiles, with many seeing significant ranking drops after Penguin's release.

How to Recover from the Google Penguin Update

Google's published guidelines provide recovery foundation.

Google Penguin Recovery Tips

1. Look for Signs of Over-Optimization

Remove high keyword density, over-used keyword phrases, and poor repetitive copywriting. Adjust headers and title tags for naturalism while reducing internal links. Recent Page Rank algorithm changes diminished anchor text links to homepages.

2. Check Anchor Text Distribution & Remove Site-Wide Links

Analyze anchor text distributions, identifying low-quality site links with poor content and low Alexa rankings. Eliminate site-wide links using keyword-rich anchor text from blogrolls and sidebars.

3. Write Better and Get More Creative: Nurture Your Content

Create balanced, impartial articles exceeding 500 words with factual information and references to authority websites, resembling Wikipedia-style entries.

4. Get Higher Quality Backlinks

Manual link building practices fade as standards evolve. When executed, keep links natural from related websites while avoiding anchor text over-use.

Regarding directories: quality, relevant directories remain acceptable within broader link-building campaigns. Avoid duplicate content snippets across business listings.

Comprehensive anchor text distribution charts demonstrate real-world impact.

What's the Future of SEO?

Beyond Google optimization fixation, numerous alternative traffic sources exist.

Alternative Measures Required: How to Optimize Without Relying on Google

1. Adopt Social Media as Core Strategy

Create shareable, differentiated content—infographics, articles, videos, badges—and distribute via Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, and relevant platforms.

2. Exchange Blog Posts

Collaborate with like-minded professionals, contributing guest posts and opinions reciprocally.

3. Buy into Social Media Advertising

Target demographics and related companies across Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn.

4. Blog & Write More Engaging Content

Produce current, relevant content through personal or professional writers.

5. Optimize Your Social Media Profiles

Maximize platform engagement, develop followings, and enhance Google+ presence.

This first-pass analysis of Penguin remains subject to ongoing tweaks and industry developments.
