Your Profile and Geo-Tagging photos

How to navigate around your profile and tagging pictures

The profile page contains several key elements. At the top sits your username. To the right is a settings icon (star-shaped) providing access to find friends, see the photos you have liked, change your privacy settings, clear your history, change your share settings, and check the support page.

Your photo count displays the total pictures uploaded. Clicking this reveals either a tiled gallery view or a scrolling list format for browsing images.

The photo map feature is a visual representation of where you have visited. Instagram only includes photos on your map when you add location data during upload (geo-tagging). Users can zoom conventionally or via buttons, with a multi-sided button linking to the tiled photo view.

Below username and photo stats are "Following" (people whose photos appear in your feed) and "Followers" (users viewing your photos).
