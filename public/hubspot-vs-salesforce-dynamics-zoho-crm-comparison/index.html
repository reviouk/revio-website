HubSpot vs Salesforce vs Dynamics 365 vs Zoho: The Honest CRM Comparison for Growing Businesses

5 April 2026

Introduction: Why Your CRM Choice Can Make or Break Your Growth

Most growing businesses reach a point where spreadsheets, sticky notes, and scattered email threads simply cannot keep up. That is the moment you start evaluating CRM platforms — and the four names that dominate every shortlist are HubSpot, Salesforce, Dynamics 365, and Zoho.

This post is not vendor marketing. It is an honest, side-by-side comparison covering real pricing, hidden costs that vendors conveniently forget to mention, setup complexity, and the total cost of ownership when you factor in everything. We have helped dozens of businesses navigate this exact decision, and the gap between what sales reps quote and what companies actually pay is staggering.

Here is the statistic that should frame every CRM conversation: 50–70% of CRM projects fail to meet their goals, and the number-one cause is not technical issues — it is poor user adoption. Your team refusing to use the system is a far bigger risk than any missing feature. Ease of use, therefore, matters more than feature lists, and that principle guides this entire comparison.

Pricing Comparison: What Each CRM Actually Costs Per User

Every vendor structures their tiers differently, so we have normalised published pricing into a single comparison for clarity.

  • Free tier: HubSpot offers £0 for 5 users, Zoho offers £0 for 3 users. Salesforce and Dynamics 365 have no free tier.
  • Entry paid tier: HubSpot Starter is £16/user/month, Salesforce Starter Suite is £20/user/month, Dynamics 365 Team Member is £6/user/month (read-only), and Zoho Standard is £11/user/month.
  • Mid tier: HubSpot Professional is £80/user/month, Salesforce Pro Suite is £80/user/month, Dynamics 365 Professional is £52/user/month, and Zoho Professional is £18/user/month.
  • Top tier: HubSpot Enterprise is £120/user/month, Salesforce Enterprise is £140/user/month, Dynamics 365 Enterprise is £76/user/month, and Zoho Enterprise is £32/user/month.
  • Premium tier: Salesforce Unlimited is £280/user/month, Dynamics 365 Premium is £108/user/month, and Zoho Ultimate is £42/user/month. HubSpot has no premium tier above Enterprise.
  • Price trend: HubSpot pricing has stayed stable. Salesforce rose 6% in 2025 with a further 5–7% expected in 2026. Dynamics 365 rose 5% in 2025. Zoho has stayed stable.

The average CRM adoption rate across all sectors is just 26%. That means nearly three-quarters of CRM users are not properly engaging with their system. HubSpot consistently wins on ease of use, which directly drives higher adoption rates. If your sales team will not log calls, update deals, or follow up on tasks inside the CRM, then every pound you spent on licensing and implementation is wasted. A CRM your team actually uses will always outperform a more powerful system that gathers dust.

Total Cost of Ownership: 10 Users, 1 Year

Licence fees are just the beginning. The real cost of a CRM includes implementation, admin staff, consultants, add-ons, and support packages. Here is what 10 users actually costs in year one.

  • HubSpot Professional: £12,000 annual licence, £1,500–£16,500 implementation, self-serve or part-time admin, no dedicated admin needed, 1–4 weeks setup time. Total year one: £18,500–£28,500.
  • Salesforce Pro Suite: £12,000 annual licence, £10,000–£50,000 implementation, £75,000–£120,000 for a dedicated admin, dedicated admin needed, 3–6 months setup time. Total year one: £46,000–£122,000.
  • Dynamics 365 Professional: £7,800 annual licence, £25,000–£75,000 implementation, partner support or 1 FTE, dedicated admin needed, 3–6 months setup time. Total year one: £42,800–£112,800.
  • Zoho Enterprise: £4,800 annual licence, £5,000–£15,000 implementation, occasional consultant, no dedicated admin needed, 1–4 weeks setup time. Total year one: £9,800–£24,800.

The difference is stark. Salesforce can cost 4–10x more than HubSpot in year one when you factor in the dedicated admin and implementation consultants that the platform requires.

Integration Ecosystems Compared

Modern businesses run on interconnected tools. How well does each CRM play with the rest of your technology stack?

  • Salesforce AppExchange: 6,200+ applications. The largest marketplace by far, covering virtually every category imaginable.
  • Dynamics 365 AppSource: 4,000+ applications. Strong within the Microsoft ecosystem, solid coverage elsewhere.
  • HubSpot Marketplace: 1,800+ applications. Covers all common integration categories — accounting, telephony, e-commerce, project management, and more.
  • Zoho Marketplace: 1,000+ extensions. Smaller ecosystem with some gaps in niche categories.

Salesforce wins on raw numbers, but context matters. Most SMBs need five to ten integrations, not six thousand. HubSpot covers all the common ones — Xero, QuickBooks, Slack, Microsoft Teams, Shopify, WordPress, Calendly, and hundreds more. For anything not natively supported, tools like Zapier and Make fill the gaps affordably. The practical difference between 1,800 and 6,200 integrations is negligible for the vast majority of growing businesses.

Who Should Choose What: Our Honest Recommendation

After working with businesses across all four platforms, here is our straightforward guidance based on real-world experience rather than theoretical feature comparisons.

Choose HubSpot If:

  • You are an SMB with 5–200 employees looking for a scalable CRM.
  • You want fast deployment measured in weeks, not months.
  • You need marketing, sales, and service functionality in a single unified platform.
  • You do not want to hire a dedicated CRM administrator.
  • You value ease of use and want your team to actually adopt the system.
  • You want predictable costs without hidden surprises.

Choose Salesforce If:

  • You are an enterprise with 500+ employees and complex multi-division requirements.
  • You need extreme customisation that justifies APEX development.
  • You have the budget for a dedicated admin (£75,000–£120,000 per year) and implementation consultants.
  • You need integrations with highly specialised enterprise software.

Choose Dynamics 365 If:

  • You are already deeply embedded in the Microsoft ecosystem — Office 365, Teams, Azure, SharePoint.
  • You need tight ERP integration with Microsoft business applications.
  • You have an existing relationship with a Microsoft-certified partner.
  • Your IT department is Microsoft-certified and comfortable with Power Platform.

Choose Zoho If:

  • You are budget-constrained and need a functional CRM at the lowest per-user cost.
  • You need a basic CRM without complex automation requirements.
  • You are comfortable with a less polished user interface and more hands-on configuration.
  • You are a very small team (under 10 people) with straightforward sales processes.

Why Businesses Are Switching to HubSpot in 2026

We are seeing a significant trend of businesses migrating away from Salesforce and Dynamics 365 to HubSpot. The reasons are remarkably consistent across industries and company sizes.

  • 40–60% total cost of ownership savings: when you factor in implementation, admin salaries, add-ons, and support contracts, HubSpot delivers dramatic savings compared to Salesforce and Dynamics.
  • Complexity fatigue: businesses are tired of paying for features they never use. Salesforce's feature set is vast, but most mid-market companies use less than 30% of what they are paying for.
  • Faster time-to-value: deploying in weeks rather than months means your team starts generating returns sooner. Every month spent in implementation is a month of lost productivity and delayed ROI.
  • Better user adoption: sales representatives actually use HubSpot. The intuitive interface reduces resistance and increases the quality of data in your system, which drives better reporting and forecasting.
  • Unified platform vs fragmented architecture: HubSpot's marketing, sales, and service tools are built on a single codebase. Salesforce's various clouds were largely acquired and bolted together, creating a fragmented user experience.
  • Proven results: companies using HubSpot's integrated tools report generating 3 times more leads and closing 94% more deals compared to using disconnected point solutions.

The shift is not about HubSpot being perfect — no CRM is. It is about HubSpot delivering the best balance of capability, usability, and value for growing businesses that do not have enterprise-scale budgets or dedicated IT departments.
