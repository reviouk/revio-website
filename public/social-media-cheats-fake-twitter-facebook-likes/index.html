Social Media Cheats: fake Twitter & Facebook Likes

11 November 2012

Like many of us who've bent the rules in board games, the digital world has its own shortcuts. A growing trend involves creating fraudulent social accounts and purchasing artificial engagement metrics. However, such tactics carry mounting risks as platforms intensify enforcement efforts.

The Scale of the Problem

Research indicates that approximately 16% of Twitter accounts were engaged in underground black hat social media techniques and had acquired over 400 fake followers on average. Even legitimate accounts occasionally accumulate artificial followers through automated bots and web crawlers.

Tools like kred.com employ sophisticated algorithms to identify questionable followers. Barracuda Labs research reveals that typical fake Twitter accounts follow over 1,700 people, suggesting unusual behavioural patterns and spam activity.

Crackdowns and Consequences

Twitter and Facebook have intensified efforts to eliminate fraudulent accounts. Your engagement metrics may decline as these accounts get removed, particularly if followers originate from unexpected geographic locations unrelated to your business focus.

Taking Action

If you suspect artificial followers, report the activity directly to the platforms. The most sustainable approach involves building genuine community support from real audiences who authentically value your brand.
